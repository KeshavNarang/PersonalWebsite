About

I am a student at Stanford Online High School, supplemented by courses from The Center for Talented Youth (CTY) and Harvard Secondary School. As a senior, I currently serve as the Teaching Assistant (TA) for Data Science, President of the Artificial Intelligence Club, Founder and Leader of the Renewable Energy Club, and Co-Captain of the Science Bowl Team. My main interests are in Entrepreneurship and Artificial Intelligence, specifically as applied to Renewable Energy technologies.

Motivated by the effects of Climate Change that I experienced firsthand, having lived in a drought most of my childhood, I created the 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, GreenTeamPower, whose mission is to equip youth with advanced scientific and political knowledge to effectively advocate for climate action. Through our podcast, TheEcoAdvocate, I engage with leading experts in every climate change discipline, from policy advisors for state senators to sustainability researchers at MIT. Based on these learnings and cutting-edge research and policies, we develop educational materials, conduct workshops in libraries and schools, and promote action through rallies and public commentary. In the last five years, we've effectively coordinated more than 20 workshops and completed 15 environmental projects across seven countries. To share our insights and maintain accessibility to knowledge, I recently authored and published a book titled "Keeping Our Planet Green: A Young Scientist's Perspective on Environmental Stewardship."

In my research, I am developing an innovative solar cell that can both create and store electricity using a perovskite and an electrochromic supercapacitor. This research will facilitate our transition to sustainable energy as it addresses the critical challenges associated with energy storage when harnessing excess solar energy: traditional solar batteries are often unaccessible for residential customers as they are costly (exceeding $10,000), heavy (over 250 lbs), and environmentally unsustainable. This research is the flagship project of the startup I co-founded ASK Solar Panel LLC, where I serve as the Chief Technology Officer. Our company is currently in the process of filing a patent application (63/456519) for our flagship product, which focuses on TiO2/WO3 nanotube-enhanced electrochromic supercapacitor integration with perovskite technology. In pursuit of our vision, we have received generous pro bono support worth $20,000 from Arent Fox for our patent application.

Since June 2023, I have been a researcher at Stanford University's Gevaert Labs, where I contribute to the development of Artificial Intelligence models for image-to-text classification. Additionally, I am an intern at Stanford University's Bhalla Labs, specializing in computational analysis of Diabetic Kidney Disease.

Sudoku

Using the Java Swing Library, I created my own rendition of the popular game Sudoku. Features of the game include the ability to load blank puzzles from a text file and save user progress so that the user can return to their game at any point in the future, even if the application was closed and reopened. The game also notifies the user if a number is placed in a square that is incorrect given the current state of the board and upon successful completion of the board, and has a hint algorithm that highlights all the potential squares in which a chosen number could appear. Try it out!

Solar Blinds

At the 2015 Management & Technology Summer Institute, my team and I designed, prototyped, and created a business plan for Solar Blinds - remote-controlled blinds that harness and are powered by solar energy. The blinds were also designed to store energy to be used for other purposes around the household, such as charging phones, or ultimately, to feed energy back into the power grid. The project received the Director's Choice Award, the highest honor given at the institute and bestowed by the two directors of the program. Some features of the prototype include motors that could raise and lower the blinds, change the angle of the slats, and increase and decrease the distance between slats via control from an Android phone using Arduino.
